# EN → DE SEO Translation & Localization Rules

## Purpose
This rule defines how English SEO content should be translated and localized into German.
The goal is NOT literal translation, but SEO-optimized German content that sounds natural and ranks well.

---

## Core Principles

1. Never translate word-by-word.
2. Always localize for German users.
3. Preserve meaning, not sentence structure.
4. German content must sound native, not translated.
5. SEO relevance in German is more important than literal accuracy.

---

## Language & Style Rules

1. Use natural, modern German.
2. Avoid English sentence structure.
3. Avoid Anglicisms unless commonly used in German.
4. Prefer:
   - Clear
   - Informative
   - Neutral
5. Avoid marketing hype or exaggerated wording.

---

## SEO Translation Rules

1. Do NOT directly translate English keywords.
2. Adapt keywords to:
   - German search behavior
   - German terminology
   - Local phrasing

Example:
- "Best camera for beginners"
  → "Beste Kamera für Einsteiger"
  (not: „Beste Kamera für Anfänger kaufen“ unless SEO requires it)

3. Titles must be rewritten, not translated.
4. Meta descriptions must be optimized separately.
5. Headings may differ from English version if it improves SEO.

---

## Structural Rules

1. Keep the same logical structure:
   - Intro
   - Features
   - Pros & Cons
   - Verdict
2. Paragraph order may be changed for better German flow.
3. Bullet points are preferred over long sentences.
4. Use shorter sentences than in English.

---

## Affiliate & Compliance Rules (Germany)

1. Avoid aggressive sales language.
2. Do NOT promise results or performance.
3. Avoid words like:
   - „garantiert“
   - „perfekt“
   - „unschlagbar“
4. Prefer neutral wording:
   - „geeignet für“
   - „kann sinnvoll sein“
   - „eine gute Option für“

5. Always assume the reader is comparison-shopping.

---

## Localization Rules

1. Currency must be in EUR (€).
2. Measurements must be metric.
3. Use German product naming where applicable.
4. Consider German consumer behavior:
   - Price-sensitive
   - Research-oriented
   - Trust-focused

---

## Output Rules

1. Output must be publish-ready.
2. No explanation of translation process.
3. No reference to English source.
4. No AI-related wording.
5. Content must look human-written.

---

## Language Priority

- Default: German
- English only if explicitly requested
- Do not mix languages in one article
