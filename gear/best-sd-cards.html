Understanding SD Card Speeds

Video Speed Class (V)

V30: 30MB/s min write ‚Äî good for 4K30
V60: 60MB/s min write ‚Äî good for 4K60
V90: 90MB/s min write ‚Äî 8K and high bitrate

UHS-I vs UHS-II

UHS-I: Up to 104MB/s ‚Äî most cameras
UHS-II: Up to 312MB/s ‚Äî pro cameras with dual slots
UHS-II cards work in UHS-I slots (at UHS-I speeds)

Capacity Sweet Spot

128GB: Great for photos + light video
256GB: Best balance for most shooters
512GB+: Heavy 4K video / long events

SD Card Tips

Buy From Authorized Sellers

Fake SD cards are everywhere. Only buy from Amazon (sold by Amazon), B&H, or authorized retailers. If the price seems too good to be true, it probably is.

Format In-Camera

Always format new cards in your camera before use. This ensures optimal file structure and compatibility.

Backup Immediately

SD cards can fail without warning. Copy files to your computer or cloud after every shoot. Don't rely on a single card for irreplaceable work.
