The Verdict

The DJI Osmo Pocket 3 is a masterpiece of engineering. It puts a massive 1-inch sensor into a tiny gimbal handle.

The low-light performance blows action cameras out of the water, and the mechanical gimbal is far smoother than electronic stabilization. The snappy rotatable screen makes vertical social content effortless.

What We Like

  • Massive 1-inch sensor = great low light quality
  • Mechanical gimbal is smoother than GoPro HyperSmooth
  • Fastest startup: Flip screen, shoot in 2 seconds
  • Face tracking is incredibly sticky

The Downsides

  • Not rugged or waterproof (needs a case)
  • Built-in mic is good, but external mic is better for wind
  • Fixed lens aperture means you need ND filters

Detailed Performance

Image Quality

The jump to a 1-inch sensor is massive. Night footage that would be mush on a phone or GoPro looks clean and professional here. Skin tones are natural and pleasing straight out of camera.

Vertical Workflows

The rotating screen isn't just a gimmick. It physically rotates the UI and changes aspect ratio, making it the fastest way to switch between YouTube (16:9) and TikTok (9:16) shooting.

Sound

Pair it with the DJI Mic 2 (which connects wirelessly without a receiver!) and you have a pro-level interview setup that fits in a jacket pocket.
